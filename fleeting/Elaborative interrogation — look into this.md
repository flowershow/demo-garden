Mentioned in [[Make It Stick — Brown, Roediger, McDaniel]] as an effective technique but the book didn't go deep.

**Elaborative interrogation** = asking "why?" and "how?" questions about the material rather than just "what?"

e.g. instead of memorising "the mitochondria produces ATP" → ask "WHY does the mitochondria produce ATP? What would happen if it didn't? How does it actually do this?"

Supposedly forces you to connect new knowledge to existing knowledge, which strengthens the memory trace.

Sounds similar to [[The Feynman Technique]] but more targeted — not about simplifying explanation, more about generating causal reasoning.
