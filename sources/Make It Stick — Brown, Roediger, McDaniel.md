*Make It Stick: The Science of Successful Learning* (2014) — Peter Brown, Henry Roediger, Mark McDaniel

The book that started this whole vault. Roediger is one of the researchers behind the testing effect studies.

## Core Argument

Most intuitive study strategies (rereading, highlighting, massed practice) are ineffective. The strategies that work feel harder and less productive in the moment — but that difficulty is the mechanism.

## Key Concepts Covered

- [[Active Recall]] — the "testing effect" chapter is the best summary I've read
- [[Spaced Repetition]] — they call it "spaced practice"
- [[Interleaving]] — chapter 4, with good empirical examples
- [[The Forgetting Curve]] — background on Ebbinghaus
- Desirable difficulties — the general principle behind all of the above

## Best Quotes

> "Learning is deeper and more durable when it's effortful."

> "Every time you retrieve a memory, you reconsolidate it — and in doing so, you can update it."

> "Trying to solve a problem before being taught the solution leads to better learning."
